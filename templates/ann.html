  • Epochs refer to the number of times an entire dataset is passed forward and backward through a neural network during training.

  • Each epoch consists of one forward pass (computing predictions), one backward pass (computing gradients and updating weights), and encompasses iterations over the entire dataset.

  • Activation functions in Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are mathematical operations applied to the output of each neuron, introducing non-linearities crucial for the network to learn complex patterns within data

  • Optimization functions in Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are algorithms that adjust the network's parameters (weights and biases) during training to minimize the error between predicted and actual outputs.
