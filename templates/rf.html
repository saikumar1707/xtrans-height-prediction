Click here to learn more about Random Forest Model

  • The number of estimators in a Random Forest model determines the quantity of decision trees aggregated to make predictions, influencing the model's predictive power.

  • Increasing the number of estimators often leads to better generalization and improved performance.

  • At a certain point, additional trees might not significantly enhance performance and could lead to longer training times.

  • Therefore, careful consideration and balancing computational resources with model effectiveness are required.
